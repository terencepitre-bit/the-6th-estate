Regulators Publish Updated List of Distressed and Underserved Rural Middle-Income Areas

Federal agencies have released an updated list identifying distressed or underserved nonmetropolitan middle-income geographies. The designation is used under Community Reinvestment Act guidelines to determine where certain bank lending and investment activities may receive favorable regulatory consideration, directing financial resources toward rural and small-town communities that face economic hardship or limited access to capital.

Why it matters: Residents and businesses in newly listed communities could find it easier to access bank loans and investment capital, as lenders have a regulatory incentive to direct financing toward these areas.

Federal Agencies Issue Joint Statement on Protecting Sensitive Data During Bank Examinations

Federal banking agencies have released a joint statement addressing how highly sensitive information should be handled during bank examinations. The guidance aims to establish clearer standards for managing confidential data collected in the examination process, reinforcing expectations for both agencies and the institutions they oversee to safeguard sensitive financial and personal information.

Why it matters: Stronger data-handling standards during bank examinations could reduce the risk that sensitive customer and institutional financial information is mishandled or exposed.

Trump Administration Set to Announce Nuclear Fuel Deal With Saudi Arabia

The Trump administration is expected to announce a deal that could allow Saudi Arabia to enrich nuclear fuel. Science / Tech / Health

Contagious Cancer Discovered in Fish for the First Time

Scientists have identified a transmissible tumor spreading among fish in a lake, marking the first known case of contagious cancer in fish. Previously, such cancers had only been documented in a small number of other animal species. The discovery raises questions about how widely transmissible cancers could exist in nature and prompts researchers to examine whether similar mechanisms could ever pose risks in other species, including humans.

Why it matters: Understanding how cancers can spread between animals could reshape what scientists thought were firm boundaries around infectious disease and cancer research, with potential implications for human health monitoring.

Sources: Deep in This Lake, a Contagious Cancer Is Spreading

Political appointee review of federal grants could reshape education research priorities

A proposed 100-page overhaul of federal grant rules would add a new review layer by political appointees, raising concerns among education research organizations. Researchers warn the change could hinder the kind of long-term, multidisciplinary work that produced the science of reading movement, which drew on decades of federally supported studies across cognitive science, neuroscience, psychology, linguistics, and classroom research.

Why it matters: If political appointees gain influence over which studies receive federal funding, the evidence base schools rely on to adopt proven teaching methods could narrow or shift away from peer-driven scientific consensus.

Sources: Education researchers warn political review of federal grants could reshape what gets studied

TreeSize shifts to subscription model, cutting off perpetual-license support renewals

TreeSize, a disk-space analysis tool, is ending support renewals for perpetual-license holders unless they switch to a subscription plan, citing current economic conditions as the driver. Users who purchased a one-time license and want continued support will need to pay recurring fees going forward. The change reflects a broader industry trend of software vendors moving away from traditional perpetual licensing.

Why it matters: Customers who paid once expecting lifetime ownership could face unexpected recurring costs or lose access to support and updates, affecting how they budget for and rely on the software.

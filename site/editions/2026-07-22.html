Regulators Identify Distressed and Underserved Rural Middle-Income Areas

Federal agencies have released an updated list of distressed or underserved nonmetropolitan middle-income geographies. These designations influence how financial institutions receive credit under community reinvestment rules for lending and investment activity in qualifying areas. Communities on the list may attract more bank lending and development activity as institutions seek regulatory credit for serving these locations.

Why it matters: Residents in designated rural areas could see increased access to loans, housing investment, and financial services as banks direct more resources toward qualifying communities.

Federal Agencies Issue Joint Guidance on Protecting Sensitive Data During Bank Examinations

Federal banking agencies have released a joint statement addressing how highly sensitive information should be handled during bank examinations. The guidance sets expectations for protecting confidential data collected in the examination process. Regulators aim to standardize practices across agencies to reduce the risk of sensitive information being mishandled or exposed during oversight reviews of financial institutions.

Why it matters: Stronger data-handling standards during bank examinations could better protect customers whose financial information is reviewed as part of the regulatory process.

Trump Administration Set to Announce Nuclear Fuel Deal With Saudi Arabia

The Trump administration is expected to announce a deal that could allow Saudi Arabia to enrich nuclear fuel as part of a civilian nuclear program. U.S. lawmakers and Israeli officials have raised opposition to the plan, warning that enrichment capability could give the kingdom a pathway toward developing nuclear weapons.

Why it matters: If Saudi Arabia gains uranium enrichment capability, it could shift the nuclear balance in the Middle East and increase pressure on other nations in the region to pursue similar programs.

Science / Tech / Health

Contagious Cancer Discovered Spreading Among Fish in Lake

Scientists have identified what appears to be a transmissible cancer spreading among fish in a lake, marking the first reported case of a contagious tumor in fish. Until now, transmissible cancers had only been documented in a small number of animal species. Researchers are examining how the disease spreads and whether similar mechanisms could exist elsewhere in nature, including questions about whether such transmission could theoretically occur in humans.

Why it matters: The discovery expands what scientists thought possible about how cancer spreads in nature, and ongoing research into transmission mechanisms could eventually shape our understanding of cancer risk across species, including humans.

Sources: Deep in This Lake, a Contagious Cancer Is Spreading

Proposed federal grant overhaul could put political appointees in charge of education research approvals

A proposed 100-page overhaul of federal grant rules would add a new review layer by political appointees over federally funded research. Education research organizations warn the change could restrict the kinds of studies that receive support, potentially limiting the broad, multi-disciplinary work that produced major advances like the science of reading movement, which drew on cognitive science, neuroscience, psychology, linguistics, and classroom research developed over decades.

Why it matters: If political review influences which studies get funded, the evidence base that schools and policymakers rely on to improve teaching and learning could narrow, affecting what educational practices reach students in classrooms.

Sources: Education researchers warn political review of federal grants could reshape what gets studied

TreeSize moves to subscription model, leaving perpetual-license holders behind

TreeSize, a disk-space analysis tool, is changing its support policy so that users who purchased perpetual licenses will no longer receive continued support unless they switch to a subscription plan. The company cited current economic conditions as the reason for the shift. Existing perpetual-license holders could lose access to updates and technical support without paying recurring fees going forward.

Why it matters: Users who paid once expecting lifetime ownership of their software could face pressure to pay ongoing subscription fees or lose access to support and updates, a pattern that could affect how people evaluate software purchases more broadly.

Sources: TreeSize won't renew perpetual-license support unless users subscribe

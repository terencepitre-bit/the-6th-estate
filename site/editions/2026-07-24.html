Oil hits $100 a barrel as Houthi attacks on Saudi tankers rattle markets

Oil surged to $100 per barrel after Iran-backed Houthi rebels attacked two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea, disrupting a critical global shipping corridor. Simultaneously, President Trump replaced a set of expiring tariffs with a new round of trade levies, adding further pressure to markets already reacting to the supply shock.

Why it matters: Rising oil prices and new tariffs could drive up costs for fuel, goods, and transportation, hitting consumers directly at the pump and in stores.

West Virginia's paid relocation program is turning temporary transplants into permanent residents

West Virginia's Ascend program, launched during COVID to attract remote workers with cash incentives, is seeing participants choose to stay long-term. The initiative is one of dozens of similar relocation programs across the country aimed at giving economically struggling regions a boost. Early results suggest financial incentives can successfully shift where remote workers choose to put down roots.

Why it matters: Remote workers considering a move could find paid relocation programs a viable way to lower living costs while states competing for residents may expand similar incentive offers.

Sources: West Virginia paid digital nomads to move there during COVID. Many are staying

Justice Dept. drops New York Times subpoenas after federal judge's sharp questioning

The Justice Department has withdrawn subpoenas demanding grand jury testimony and phone records from New York Times journalists. The decision came after a federal judge questioned the subpoenas sharply during a Thursday hearing. The move ends an immediate legal confrontation between federal prosecutors and the Times over compelled journalist cooperation with a grand jury investigation.

Why it matters: Journalists at major outlets could face less pressure to hand over source information or testify before grand juries, at least in the near term.

Musk Says He Is Unbothered by Public Criticism Amid Growing AI Concerns

Elon Musk, speaking to The Economist, said he does not care if people hate him and denied being a racist. His comments came as fears around artificial intelligence continue to mount. Musk, who has been a prominent voice on AI development, struck a defiant tone, describing the current moment with the phrase 'let's enjoy the ride' as scrutiny of both his views and the technology intensifies.

Why it matters: As one of the most influential figures shaping AI development and public policy, Musk's attitudes and statements could influence how quickly guardrails on powerful technologies are pursued or abandoned.

Education

Schools tackle writing instruction as the next frontier in literacy reform

As reading instruction has gained renewed focus in recent years, educators are turning attention to a parallel gap: students are not being taught to write effectively. At Sand Creek Elementary in Highlands Ranch, Colorado, teachers are implementing structured writing lessons beginning in kindergarten and first grade, with early results showing measurable growth. Experts suggest writing has long been undertaught in American classrooms, leaving students underprepared for academic and professional demands.

Why it matters: Students who never receive explicit writing instruction could struggle to communicate clearly in higher education and the workforce, affecting their long-term economic and academic opportunities.

Sources: The next literacy challenge: Teaching students how to write
Real Estate

Rayner Rules Out Rent Controls in England

Housing Secretary Angela Rayner has told the BBC that rent controls will not be introduced in England, arguing the Renters Rights Act is already having a significant impact on the housing market. The decision closes off a policy option that tenant advocates had pushed for as a tool to curb rising rents, signalling the government's approach will rely on existing tenancy reforms rather than direct price intervention.

Why it matters: Renters facing rising housing costs could find their options for relief limited to protections already established under the Renters Rights Act, with no government cap on how much landlords can charge.

Sources: Angela Rayner rules out rent controls in England

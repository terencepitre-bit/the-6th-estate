Richard Jewell's False Accusation in the 1996 Atlanta Olympics Bombing Revisited 30 Years Later

Security guard Richard Jewell, who initially helped save lives by spotting a suspicious backpack at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics, was publicly named the primary bombing suspect before being cleared. His friend and attorney reflects on how the false accusation derailed Jewell's life and reputation. The case remains a landmark example of media and law enforcement rushing to judgment before an investigation was complete.

Why it matters: The Jewell case illustrates how premature public accusations — amplified by media — could permanently damage an innocent person's career, mental health, and standing in their community before any verdict is reached.
